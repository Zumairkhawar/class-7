Nature Of Pakistan

Mountain Ranges:


Pakistan is home to some of the world's highest peaks, including K2 (the second highest mountain in the world), Nanga Parbat, and the Karakoram Range. These mountains attract climbers and trekkers from around the globe.

Lakes and Rivers:


The country boasts several picturesque lakes such as Lake Saiful Muluk, Lake Attabad, and the Satpara Lake. Rivers like the mighty Indus, Jhelum, Chenab, and Ravi flow through its fertile plains.

Deserts:


The Thar Desert, located in the southeastern part of the country, is one of the largest deserts in the world. Its vast stretches of sand dunes and sparse vegetation are characteristic of this region.

Forests and Wildlife:


The country's diverse ecosystems support a variety of wildlife, including endangered species such as the snow leopard, markhor, and the Indus river dolphin. National parks and reserves like the Hingol National Park and Deosai National Park are important for conservation efforts.

Climate:


Pakistan experiences a range of climates from arid in the desert regions to temperate in the northern mountains. The monsoon season brings rainfall to much of the country, essential for agriculture.
